# Hypertrophy Training Philosophy

## The Goal: Look Huge, Not Just Strong

This program is optimized for **aesthetic muscle gain** — the goal is to look big and muscular, not to maximize 1-rep-max numbers. These are related but not the same thing. Here’s what that means in practice:

## Core Principles

### 1. Time Under Tension (TUT)
The 3-second eccentric (slow lowering phase) is not optional. Research consistently shows that controlled eccentrics increase muscle fiber micro-damage and subsequent growth compared to dropping the weight fast.

Every rep: **3 seconds down, 1 second up** (for compounds). Isolation work: **3 seconds down, 1-2 seconds squeeze at peak contraction**.

### 2. Mind-Muscle Connection
For compound lifts (bench, squat, deadlift) — think about the primary muscle doing the work, not just moving the weight from A to B.
- Bench press: feel your chest stretching on the way down, driving the bar through your chest on the way up
- Lat pulldown: think about pulling your elbows to your hips, not pulling the bar down
- Leg press: feel your quads contracting through the full ROM, don’t just lock out your knees

### 3. Progressive Overload
You must lift more over time. The trigger for muscle growth is progressive mechanical tension.

**Rule:** When you hit the **top of the rep range** for **all sets**, add 5 lb next session.
- Example: Bench Press 4×8 at 135 lb → next session try 140 lb
- If you can only get 6 reps at 140 lb, stay there until you can get 4×8
- Track your weights using the set log on each workout page

### 4. Rep Range Strategy
- **6-8 reps** (compound anchors): mechanical tension focus — this is your strength builder
- **8-12 reps** (secondary compounds): balanced tension + metabolic stress
- **12-20 reps** (isolation/pump finishers): metabolic stress + cellular swelling (the “pump”)

All three mechanisms contribute to hypertrophy. The program uses all three.

### 5. The Pump Is Real
Late-session isolation work (cable flyes, lateral raises, curls) causes muscular swelling from accumulated metabolic byproducts. This isn’t just “feel-good” — the cellular swelling is a hypertrophy signal. This is why every session ends with higher-rep pump work.

### 6. Shoulder Health (Face Pulls)
Face pulls appear 3×/week across Days 1, 2, and 4. This is intentional. Heavy pressing volume creates anterior shoulder dominance over time. Face pulls train the external rotators and rear delts, keeping the joint balanced. **Never skip face pulls.**

### 7. Calves Need Dedicated Work
Calves are stubborn for most people because they’re trained constantly in daily walking (high endurance stimulus). To grow them:
- Train through **full range of motion** (all the way up on the raise, all the way down for a deep stretch)
- Use both **standing raises** (gastrocnemius — the visible outer calf) and **seated raises** (soleus — the deeper inner calf that pushes the gastrocnemius outward)
- 2×/week, 14+ sets total

### 8. Forearms Follow a Similar Rule
Forearm flexors are trained implicitly on every pulling exercise. The gap is the **forearm extensors** — trained by reverse curls and wrist extensions. Neglecting extensors causes forearm imbalance and elbow pain over time. Day 2 and Day 4 both include dedicated forearm extensor work.

## Lean Bulk Nutrition
See `nutrition/calorie-targets.html` for the full math.
- **+300 kcal/day surplus** on workout days — enough to fuel muscle growth without excessive fat gain
- **+100 kcal/day** on rest days — maintenance-adjacent
- **Rate of gain:** target 0.5–1 lb/week — anything faster is mostly fat
- **Protein:** 154g/day (1.15g/lb) — the single most important dietary variable for muscle growth

## Progress Check (Monthly)
Weigh yourself on Monday morning before eating. Track the trend:
- Week 4: target ~136 lb (+2 lb)
- Week 8: target ~138-139 lb (+4-5 lb)
- Week 12: target ~141-142 lb (+7-8 lb)
- Week 24 (6 months): target 150+ lb (+16+ lb)

If you’re gaining slower than this: add 200 kcal/day.
If you’re gaining faster than 1.5 lb/week: reduce 150 kcal/day.
