Bulgarian Split Squat

3 × 8-10 each leg
Rest: 2 min
Muscles🟢 VMO + Glute Med (inner quad teardrop) · 🔵 Quads · 🔵 Hip Flexors

Rear foot elevated on bench, front foot far enough forward that shin stays vertical. Lower until front thigh is parallel. The elevated rear foot increases front-leg loading and stretch — VMO and glute medius take more load here than in a back squat. Do all reps one leg, then switch. Hold DBs or barbell.

Barbell Hip Thrust

3 × 10-12 reps
Rest: 2 min
Muscles🟢 Glute Max (peak contraction) · 🔵 Hamstrings · 🔵 Adductors

Shoulders on bench, bar padded across hips. Drive hips up to full extension — squeeze glutes hard at the top for 1 second. The hip thrust is the best glute max exercise because it maximally loads the muscle in its shortened position (fully contracted). RDL loads the stretch; hip thrust loads the contraction. Both are needed.

Equipment not available? See alternatives
DB Hip ThrustDumbbell on hips instead of barbell. Same sets/reps.
Glute Bridge (floor)No bench needed. Less ROM but same muscle. 3×15-20.
Cable Pull-ThroughFace away from cable stack, hinge and thrust. 3×12-15.

Chin-Up (supinated grip)

3 × 6-10 reps
Rest: 2 min
Muscles🟢 Upper Lats · 🟢 Bicep Short Head · 🔵 Rear Delt

Underhand (supinated) grip shoulder-width. Full hang at the bottom — full lat stretch. Pull chin above bar, squeeze at top. The supinated grip recruits the bicep short head more than a pronated pull-up, making this a back + bicep compound. Add a weight belt when you can do 10+ reps with ease.

Equipment not available? See alternatives
Assisted Chin-Up MachineBuild toward bodyweight. Same sets/reps.
Close-Grip Lat PulldownUnderhand grip, pull to upper chest. Same sets/reps.
Resistance Band Chin-UpBand around knees for assistance. Same sets/reps.

Decline Dumbbell Press

3 × 8-10 reps
Rest: 2 min
Muscles🟢 Lower Chest (costal head) · 🔵 Tricep · 🔵 Anterior Delt

Bench declined 15-30°. The decline angle shifts load to the costal (lower) pec head and allows the most weight of all press angles. Mon = flat (mid chest), Tue = incline (upper), Thu = decline (lower). Full weekly chest coverage across three different portions. Press to lockout, control the descent.

Equipment not available? See alternatives

Decline Barbell PressBarbell version. Same sets/reps.

Reverse Curl (EZ-bar)

3 × 10-12 reps
Rest: 90 sec
Muscles🟢 Brachioradialis (forearm/elbow thickness) · 🔵 Brachialis · 🔵 Wrist Extensors

Overhand (pronated) grip on EZ-bar. Curl with wrists neutral — don't let them drop. Brachioradialis is the thick muscle that runs from the outer elbow down the forearm; it's only maximally recruited in a pronated curl. Thick forearms are visible even with sleeves — often the most noticed muscle.

Equipment not available? See alternatives
DB Reverse CurlOverhand grip DBs. Same sets/reps.
Cable Reverse CurlStraight bar, overhand. Constant tension. 3×12-15.
Zottman CurlCurl up supinated, rotate to pronated on the way down. Same sets/reps.

Skull Crusher (EZ-bar)

3 × 10-12 reps
Rest: 2 min
Muscles🟢 Tricep Long Head (loaded stretch) · 🔵 Tricep Medial Head

Lie flat, EZ-bar above forehead. Elbows fixed pointing up — only the elbow joint moves. Lower bar toward forehead (or just past it for more stretch) in 3 seconds. The supine position with arms overhead puts maximum stretch on the long head. Paired with Tuesday's overhead ext, the long head gets two weekly overloaded-stretch sessions for maximum growth.

Equipment not available? See alternatives

Cable Skull CrusherFloor pulley, lie on bench. Constant tension. 3×12-15.
Close-Grip Bench PressHands shoulder-width, lower to chest. 3×10-12.

Seated Calf Raise

3 × 15-20 reps
Rest: 60 sec
Muscles🟢 Soleus (deep calf thickness)

Knee bent 90°, full ROM with a 2-sec pause at the bottom stretch. The soleus is active when the knee is bent — the gastrocnemius (worked on Mon/Wed/Fri standing) is slack in this position. Thursday repeats the seated variation for a second weekly soleus session.
