Conventional Deadlift

4 × 4-6 reps
Rest: 3-4 min
Muscles🟢 Hamstrings  ·  🟢 Glutes  ·  🟢 Erectors  ·  🔵 Quads  ·  🔵 Traps

Hinge at the hips, not your back. Bar stays in contact with shins on the way up. Brace your core before every rep as if you're about to take a punch. Drive the floor away from you. Lower in 3 seconds under control. This is the single highest-yield mass builder — it triggers full-body growth hormone release.

Trap Bar DeadliftEasier on the lower back. Identical stimulus. Same sets/reps.

Cable Pull-ThroughGreat for learning the hip hinge. Anchor at ankle height. 3×15.

Barbell Hip Thrust

3 × 10-12 reps
Rest: 2 min
Muscles🟢 Glutes (max)  ·  🔵 Hamstrings  ·  🔵 Hip Flexors

Upper back rests on a bench, barbell across your hips with a pad. Feet flat on floor, hip-width. Drive hips straight up — squeeze glutes hard at the top for 1 second. Lower in 3 seconds. Glutes create the athletic, powerful look and protect your lower back and knees.

Equipment not available? See alternatives
Smith Machine Hip ThrustMore stable. Set bar at the right height. Same sets/reps.
Cable Pull-Through (glute focus)Low cable, hinge forward, squeeze glutes at the top. 3×15.
Bodyweight Single-Leg Hip ThrustOn bench, one leg raised. 3×15 per side.

Lying Leg Curl

3 × 12-15 reps
Rest: 90 sec
Muscles🟢 Hamstrings  ·  🔵 Calves (gastrocnemius)

Lie face down. Curl heels toward glutes — stop at 90° unless machine allows more. Control the eccentric (return) in 3 seconds; this phase builds the most size. Don't let your hips lift off the pad — that's your lower back compensating. Direct hamstring isolation you can't get from the deadlift alone.

Equipment not available? See alternatives
Seated Leg CurlLonger hamstring stretch. Same sets/reps.
Nordic Hamstring CurlBrutal bodyweight option. Hook feet under something. 3×6-8 slow reps.
Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift (DB)Balance + hamstring. 3×10 per side.

Seated Calf Raise

4 × 15-20 reps
Rest: 90 sec
Muscles🟢 Soleus (deep calf)  ·  🔵 Gastrocnemius

The bent-knee position shuts off the gastrocnemius and isolates the soleus — the muscle that gives calves their width when viewed from behind. Full stretch at the bottom (heel drops below pad level), press to full tippy-toe contraction, hold 1 second. You hit standing (gastrocnemius) on Day 3; this targets what standing can't reach.

Equipment not available? See alternatives
DB Seated Calf RaiseSit on a bench, dumbbell on each knee. Same sets/reps.
Leg Press Calf Raise (toes only)Use the leg press foot platform. 4×20.
Banded Seated Calf RaiseAnchor band at foot for resistance. 4×25.

Core Circuit

2 rounds
Rest: 60 sec between rounds
Muscles🟢 Rectus Abdominis  ·  🟢 Transverse Abdominis  ·  🔵 Hip Flexors

A) Plank — 45 seconds: Forearms on floor, straight line head to heel. Brace as if expecting a punch. Don't let hips drop or pike up. B) Hanging Leg Raise — 12 reps: Hang from bar, raise straight (or bent) legs to hip height. Lower in 3 seconds. No rest between A and B — rest 60 sec between rounds. A strong core stabilizes your spine for every other lift in this plan.

Equipment not available? See alternatives
Ab Wheel RolloutKneel, roll forward until arms fully extended, pull back. 2×10.
Cable CrunchKneel at high cable, crunch elbows to knees. 3×15.
Dead BugLie on back, opposite arm/leg lower to floor. 2×10 per side.
